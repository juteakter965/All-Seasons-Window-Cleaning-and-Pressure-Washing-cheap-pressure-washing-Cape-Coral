Common Mistakes When Choosing Cheap Pressure Washing

Avoid these pitfalls when searching for budget-friendly pressure washing services:

1. Choosing the Absolute Cheapest Without Research

If a price seems too good to be true, it probably is. Extremely low prices may indicate:

  • Unlicensed or uninsured operators
  • Use of improper pressure settings that damage surfaces
  • Inadequate equipment or cleaning solutions
  • Inexperienced technicians
  • Poor customer service or no guarantees

2. Ignoring Online Reviews

Even with cheap prices, companies should maintain positive customer reviews. Check:

  • Google Reviews
  • Facebook recommendations
  • Better Business Bureau ratings
  • Nextdoor neighborhood endorsements

3. Not Getting a Written Estimate

Always insist on written quotes that detail:

  • Exact services to be performed
  • Total cost including all fees
  • Payment terms and methods accepted
  • Guarantee or warranty information
  • Timeline for completion

4. Skipping Insurance Verification

Cheap doesn't mean uninsured. Verify that any pressure washing company carries:

  • General liability insurance
  • Workers' compensation insurance
  • Proof of proper licensing

Critical Warning

Never hire unlicensed or uninsured pressure washing services, no matter how cheap. If something goes wrong—property damage, personal injury, or poor results—you'll have no recourse and could face significant costs to fix problems.

5. Falling for Bait-and-Switch Tactics

Some companies advertise extremely low base prices then add numerous surcharges:

  • "Difficult access" fees
  • Equipment charges
  • Cleaning solution upgrades
  • Travel or fuel surcharges
  • Weekend or after-hours fees

Always confirm that quotes are all-inclusive.
